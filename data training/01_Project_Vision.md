---

# Document Purpose

This document defines the long-term vision, philosophy, experience, and strategic objectives of the project.

It should be considered the primary source of truth before any design, development, or content migration begins.

Every design decision, interaction, content structure, and technical implementation should align with this document.

---

# Project Overview

The goal is to create a timeless digital archive for Vietnamese contemporary artist **Ngo Thi Thuy Duyen**.

This project is not intended to be a traditional portfolio website.

It is not a commercial gallery.

It is not an online shop.

It is not a corporate profile.

It is not simply a personal website.

Instead, it is designed as an evolving artistic archive that grows together with the artist throughout her lifetime.

The website itself should become an extension of the artistic practice.

Visitors should not feel they are browsing webpages.

They should feel they are slowly entering a living archive of memories, artworks, journeys, research, language, and transformation.

---

# Core Vision

The archive exists to preserve artistic consciousness rather than merely document artistic production.

Every artwork represents only one visible layer.

Behind every artwork exist invisible layers:

- Memory
- Observation
- Research
- Travel
- Conversation
- Language
- Silence
- Material
- Time
- Transformation

The website should reveal these layers gradually.

Nothing should feel rushed.

Nothing should feel overexplained.

Mystery is intentional.

Silence is meaningful.

Absence is part of the experience.

---

# Mission Statement

To create one of the most thoughtful digital archives for a contemporary artist in Southeast Asia.

The archive should preserve not only completed artworks but also the invisible creative process behind them.

The website should remain meaningful twenty years from now.

It should become more valuable as it grows.

# Core Philosophy

Art does not exist separately from life.

The archive should never separate:

- artwork
- memory
- travel
- observation
- language
- research
- process

Every section should naturally connect with another.

# Guiding Principles

## Principle 1

Art comes before technology.

Technology exists only to support artistic expression.

---

## Principle 2

Slow exploration is preferred over fast consumption.

Visitors should discover rather than scan.

---

## Principle 3

Whitespace is content.

Silence has visual value.

Avoid unnecessary decoration.

---

## Principle 4

Minimalism should never feel empty.

Every empty space should feel intentional.

---

## Principle 5

The archive should remain unfinished.

Growth is part of its identity.

---

## Principle 6

The interface should disappear.

Visitors should focus on the artworks rather than the website.

---

## Principle 7

Beauty should emerge through restraint.

Never add elements simply because they are possible.

---

# Storytelling Philosophy

Instead of telling visitors everything immediately,

allow them to discover relationships naturally.

One artwork may reveal:

- sketches
- notes
- conversations
- places
- historical references
- travel journals
- exhibition texts

This creates a richer narrative.

---

# Content Strategy

Every piece of content should answer at least one of these questions:

What was created?

Why was it created?

Where did it begin?

How did it evolve?

What memories influenced it?

What materials were involved?

How does it connect to other works?

If the content cannot answer any of these questions, reconsider including it.

---

# Success Criteria

The project is successful when:

- Visitors spend time exploring rather than quickly leaving.
- Relationships between artworks become discoverable.
- The archive remains easy to expand.
- Curators can research efficiently.
- The artist can maintain the archive independently.
- The website still feels contemporary after many years.

This project is not about building a website.

Frameworks will evolve.
